🌽 WI Agricultural Forecasting Advisory System

Understanding the crop diseases behind Wisconsin's daily risk forecasts

Know the risk before it costs you a harvest

This advisory system turns local weather from the Wisconsin weather-station network into a daily, easy-to-read disease-risk forecast for corn and soybean — so you can time scouting and fungicide decisions with confidence.

Open the forecasting dashboard Learn about the diseases

How the forecast works

Every day the system pulls temperature, humidity, and other readings from nearby weather stations and runs them through disease-risk models built by UW–Madison plant pathologists. You get a simple risk level for each field crop — no spreadsheets required.

1

Local weather in

Hourly readings come from the Wisconet network of Wisconsin weather stations.

2

Models run daily

Research-based models estimate how favorable conditions are for each disease to develop.

3

Risk level out

Each station gets a Low, Moderate, or High risk rating you can see on a map at a glance.

4

You decide

Use the rating to prioritize scouting and time management decisions like fungicide applications.

Reading the risk levels

Risk levels describe how favorable the weather has been for disease — not a guarantee that disease is present. Always confirm with in-field scouting.

Low risk Conditions are unfavorable for the disease. Keep scouting on your normal schedule.
Moderate risk Conditions are becoming favorable. Scout more closely and watch the trend.
High risk Conditions strongly favor the disease. Prioritize scouting and consider management.

The diseases we forecast

Each model targets a specific field-crop disease. Tap any card for the full, research-backed write-up from the Crop Protection Network.

Corn

Tar Spot

A fungal disease that shows up as small, raised black spots on leaves. It thrives in cool, humid, wet weather and can cut yields sharply when it arrives early and spreads up the plant.

Read more
Corn

Gray Leaf Spot

One of the most common corn diseases. Rectangular gray-to-tan lesions run between leaf veins. Warm temperatures and long periods of high humidity or leaf wetness drive infection.

Read more
Soybean

Frogeye Leaf Spot

Named for its round, gray-centered spots with reddish-brown borders. Warm, humid weather favors it, and susceptible varieties can lose significant leaf area and yield.

Read more
Soybean

White Mold

A cool-weather disease that strikes during flowering, producing fluffy white growth on stems. Risk depends on the canopy, row spacing, and irrigation — the dashboard lets you pick the scenario that matches your field.

Read more
Cover crop

Cereal Rye Biomass

Not a disease — this model estimates how much cereal rye cover-crop biomass you've grown. Hitting the right biomass threshold drives benefits like weed suppression and soil protection.
